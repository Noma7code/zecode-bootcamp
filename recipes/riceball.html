Groundnut Soup with Rice Balls

Description

This is one of Ghanaian's favourite meals. It is delicious, warming, savoury and guaranteed to be one of your favourite Ghanaian dishes too. It is often made with poultry, stewing hen and smoked turkey but you can definitely make this with other meats like beed, lam, goat etc.

Ingredients

For the Groundnut Soup

  • Protein:
    • 2 lbs chicken (cut into pieces, preferably mature/stewing chicken)
    • 1 lb smoked turkey (cut into pieces)
  • Base & Aromatics:
    • 2 large tomatoes
    • 2 tbsp tomato paste
    • 2 large onions
    • 4-inch piece of ginger (peeled)
    • 2 tsp anise seed
    • 5 garlic cloves
    • 3 scotch bonnet peppers (or to taste)
    • 2 additional scotch bonnets (for garnish)
  • Peanut Mix:
    • 1½ cups peanut butter
  • Seasoning:
    • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional Add-ins:
    • Boiled okra
    • Oyster mushrooms
    • Button mushrooms
  • Water as needed

For the Rice Balls

  • 2 cups long-grain/jasmine rice
  • 3½ cups water
  • 1 tsp salt

Instructions

To Make the Soup

  1. Season chicken with salt and pepper. Let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Blend 1 onion, 3 garlic cloves, ginger, and 2 scotch bonnets with about ⅓ cup water.
  3. Add chicken and smoked turkey to a large pot. Pour in the blended mixture and anise seed. Mix well.
  4. Cover and steam on low heat for 30 minutes until chicken is cooked.
  5. In another pot, boil 2 tomatoes, 1 onion, 2 garlic cloves, and 1 scotch bonnet for 30 minutes.
  6. Blend the boiled mixture and strain it into the pot with the chicken and turkey.
  7. In a saucepan, mix peanut butter, tomato paste, and 1 cup water. Heat on low until smooth and slightly thickened, stirring constantly.
  8. Pour peanut mixture into the soup pot. Simmer on low for 30-40 minutes until oil surfaces.
  9. Add extra whole scotch bonnets for extra flavor (optional).
  10. If soup is too thick, add hot water. Adjust seasoning to taste.

To Make Rice Balls

  1. Rinse rice and add it to a pot with salt and water. Bring to a boil.
  2. Reduce heat to low, cover, and cook until all water is absorbed (15-30 minutes).
  3. Once cooked, mash rice with a wooden spoon inside the pot.
  4. Wet a bowl slightly, scoop rice in, and swirl bowl to shape rice into a ball.
  5. Use clean hands to form smoother balls if needed. Serve with soup or wrap for later use.
  6. To reheat, microwave or steam rice balls (ensure plastic wrap is sealed if using boiling water).
